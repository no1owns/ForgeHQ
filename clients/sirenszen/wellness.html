Feel Better Every Day

Self Care & Wellness

Your massage session is just the beginning. What you do between visits — how you move, what you eat, how you restore — shapes how your body holds and releases tension over time. Here are some therapist-recommended practices to help you maintain your results and feel your best every day.

Stretches & Movement

Movement for every body

Regular stretching between sessions helps you hold on to the benefits of your treatment longer — improving flexibility, reducing everyday tension, and keeping your muscles from tightening back up. These are five of our favorite therapist-recommended stretches you can do right at home or at your desk.

Neck Rolls

Great for releasing the tension that builds from screen time, stress, and long commutes. Slowly drop your chin toward your chest, then roll your head in a full circle — three to four times in each direction. Keep the movement slow and intentional, never forcing the range of motion.

Doorway Chest Stretch

If you spend hours at a desk or phone, this one is essential. Stand in a doorway with one forearm resting against the frame, elbow at shoulder height. Gently turn your body away until you feel a stretch across your chest and front shoulder. Hold 30–45 seconds each side — it directly counters rounded, hunched posture.

Upper Trap Stretch

This stretch targets the neck and shoulder muscles that tend to tighten right back up after a massage if left unaddressed. Sit or stand tall, tilt your head gently toward one shoulder, and apply light downward pressure with your hand. Hold 30 seconds each side. Do it daily to maintain the release your session created.

Wrist Flexor Stretch

Essential for anyone who types, texts, or works with their hands. Extend one arm straight in front of you with your palm facing up, then use your other hand to gently pull your fingers back toward your body. Hold 20–30 seconds. Switch sides. Your wrists and forearms will thank you.

Seated Spinal Twist

A simple but powerful move for lower back tension. Sit tall in a chair with both feet flat on the floor. Place your right hand on your left knee and gently rotate your torso to the left, using the breath to deepen the twist on each exhale. Hold 20–30 seconds, then switch sides. This relieves compression and improves spinal mobility.

Nutrition & Foods

Fuel that supports recovery

What you eat has a direct impact on how your body holds tension, recovers from stress, and responds to massage therapy. Anti-inflammatory foods can reduce muscle soreness, support circulation, and help your body stay in the relaxed state your session worked hard to create.

Leafy Greens
Spinach, kale, Swiss chard

Rich in magnesium, which helps relax tight muscles and prevent cramping. Easy to add to a morning smoothie, a salad, or a stir-fry — your muscles feel the difference.

Blueberries
Fresh or frozen

Packed with antioxidants that actively reduce inflammation and speed up muscle recovery. A handful a day goes a long way for your body's repair process.

Avocado
Add to salads, toast, or smoothies

High in potassium and heart-healthy fats that reduce inflammation and support proper muscle function. One of the most nutrient-dense whole foods you can add to your routine.

Turmeric
Golden milk, soups, rice dishes

Contains curcumin, one of nature's most studied anti-inflammatory compounds. Pair with a pinch of black pepper to dramatically increase absorption. Warming and deeply nourishing.

Salmon & Omega-3s
Salmon, sardines, walnuts, flaxseed

Omega-3 fatty acids reduce exercise- and stress-induced inflammation and support long-term joint health. Aim for two servings of fatty fish per week, or supplement with a quality fish oil.

Almonds
Raw, as almond butter, or almond milk

High in magnesium and vitamin E, both of which protect against inflammation and muscle spasms. A small handful makes an ideal post-session snack.

Tart Cherry Juice
100% tart cherry, unsweetened

Clinically studied for its ability to reduce muscle soreness and accelerate strength recovery after physical activity. Most effective when consumed within an hour of exercise or a session.

Water
Still, sparkling, herbal teas

Hydration is foundational. After a massage, your body is actively flushing metabolic waste from your muscles. Aim for 2–3 liters daily — a little more on session days — to support circulation and prevent fatigue.

Recipes

Nourishing meals and drinks

Simple, whole-ingredient recipes you can make in minutes to support your body between sessions. These two smoothies are a great place to start — each one packs anti-inflammatory ingredients that work with your body's natural recovery process.

Anti-Inflammatory Golden Smoothie
Ingredients
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 frozen banana
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp fresh or ground ginger
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • Pinch of black pepper (activates the turmeric)
How to Make It

Add all ingredients to a blender and blend until smooth and creamy. Serve immediately. Best enjoyed in the morning before or after light movement — it warms the body from the inside out and sets an anti-inflammatory tone for the day.

Recovery Blueberry Almond Smoothie
Ingredients
  • 1 cup almond milk
  • ½ cup frozen blueberries
  • 1 handful fresh spinach
  • 1 tbsp almond butter
  • 1 tsp honey
  • ½ frozen banana
How to Make It

Blend until smooth and creamy. The spinach disappears into the blueberry color, making this an easy way to get your greens in without the taste. Perfect post-massage or post-workout — the antioxidants go to work right away to reduce soreness and replenish what your muscles need.
