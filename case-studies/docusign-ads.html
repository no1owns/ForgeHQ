Overview

Making DocuSign Unmissable

DocuSign was already a household name in enterprise contracts — but the brand wanted to expand its reach beyond legal teams into broader consumer and SMB audiences. The assignment: develop a digital ad campaign that felt more human, more urgent, and more culturally relevant than the typical SaaS ad playbook.

I researched the platform, studied the available digital advertising surfaces, and developed a creative strategy built around two core taglines: "Just DocuSign It" and "Close Business Fast" — each designed to speak to a different segment of the target audience.

Challenge

SaaS Marketing That Doesn't Feel Like SaaS

The challenge with established B2B brands expanding into consumer markets is that corporate creative habits die hard. DocuSign's marketing had historically spoken in feature bullets and enterprise language — which worked for procurement teams but not for the person just trying to sign their lease or close their first client.

The campaign needed to feel punchy, culturally aware, and immediate — without losing the trust and professionalism the brand had built over years.

Approach

Leading With the Feeling, Not the Feature

"Just DocuSign It" borrowed cultural shorthand from the world of action-oriented slogans — putting DocuSign in the same instinctive category as other verbs people use without thinking. The goal was to make signing feel as automatic as searching.

"Close Business Fast" spoke directly to the ambitious SMB owner who knows that speed-to-signature is a competitive advantage. Both campaigns ran across display, social, and pre-roll video formats.
