Overview

Where Film Meets Healing

Filmmakers Therapy Couch started with a simple, urgent question: where do filmmakers go when the work is hard and the industry is harder? The answer became a platform — part podcast, part documentary, part community — built around the idea that creative work and mental wellness are not separate conversations.

The project documents Hip Hop at Harvard, brings together filmmakers, creatives, entrepreneurs, and industry leaders, and creates a safe space for stories that the mainstream film industry often overlooks.

The Work

Six Episodes. Real Conversations.

The Talk Series features intimate conversations with filmmakers and storytellers — women, Black women, and people of colour — who are using their creative force to transform the media landscape. Quick tips and real talk, best enjoyed with a cup of tea.

Beyond the podcast, FTC has documented events including The Last Poets at The Apollo, built the Little Scholars Cinema Train program in partnership with schools, and developed training in DEIB storytelling and impact producing strategies.

Impact

Shifting the Narrative

FTC exists at the intersection of storytelling, mental health, and cultural legacy. The Harvard connection brought academic credibility to a conversation often dismissed as too niche — and proved that the stories being told in community spaces deserve the same rigour and visibility as any major studio production.

Every event, episode, and partnership is built around the same belief: empowering storytellers to think big and take action.
